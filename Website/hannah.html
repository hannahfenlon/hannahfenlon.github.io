About Me

On the first day of my sophomore year of high school, I walked into my Intro to Java Programming course, so excited to dive into the world of computer science. But looking around at my classmates, that excitement quickly turned to apprehension. In a class of almost 30 students, I was one of just three girls. Since then, I’ve often been the only girl in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) classes, and in leadership positions. As the first female Student Body President elected in 20 years at my suburban New York high school, I personally understand that achieving gender equality in government and academics is a complex challenge. Now, as a freshman at Columbia University, my passions for technology and female empowerment have only continued to grow. I have experienced firsthand the significance of advancing female empowerment through the Girls Who Code (GWC) summer computer science program at Pfizer’s NYC headquarters. GWC transformed the traditionally male-dominated tech world into a supportive, female community for me. At Columbia, I have explored the intersections of technology and scientific innovation in diverse industries in NYC through an entrepreneurship club on campus.
